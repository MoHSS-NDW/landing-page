Acknowledging Our Partners in Advancing Healthcare Data in Namibia

At the Ministry of Health and Social Services (MHSS) in Namibia, our commitment to improving healthcare outcomes and strengthening data-driven decision-making is unwavering. We understand that achieving our goals requires the collaborative efforts of dedicated partners who share our vision for a healthier Namibia. We are proud to acknowledge the invaluable support and contributions of our funding and development partners:

  • PEPFAR (the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief)
  • CDC (the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention)
  • UCSF (the University of California, San Francisco)
  • UNAIDS (the Joint United Nations Programme on HIV/AIDS)
PEPFAR has been instrumental in our fight against the HIV/AIDS epidemic, CDC has guided our data-driven decisions, UCSF has expertly coordinated the design and development of our Health Information Systems including National Data Warehouse tools, and UNAIDS has provided global leadership in our quest to combat HIV/AIDS. Together, these partnerships have been pivotal in shaping a healthier future for Namibia.
